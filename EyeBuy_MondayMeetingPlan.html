Step 1 — Form the Business

What is an LLC?
An LLC (Limited Liability Company) is the legal structure that makes Eye Buy an official business. It separates your personal money from the business — so if Eye Buy ever has a legal problem, your personal bank account is protected. You need this before taking real money from customers.

Where do we form it?

Raj is in NJ. Jason is in MN. You can form an LLC in any state — you don't have to live there. For simplicity, form it in New Jersey since Raj is handling the filing. It's the cheapest and easiest path right now.

New Jersey (recommended)
  • ~$125 to file, $75/yr to renew
  • Raj handles everything locally
  • Simple to manage, no surprises
  • Right choice before raising money
Minnesota
  • ~$155 to file, no annual fee
  • Only worth it if Jason handles most ops
  • Slightly more admin for Raj
  • Similar cost long-term

Step 3 — The Website & Payments

Quick Overview
The prototype Raj built is a website that lives on the internet. Right now it looks like a real store but can't take payments. The next step is connecting a payment system so buyers can pay and sellers get paid.
What We're Using (keep it)
  • The prototype website Raj built
  • Vercel — where the website is hosted online
  • Stripe Connect — the payment system we're adding
  • Cloudflare Stream — for seller videos
  • Total: ~$30–50/mo to run
Shopify — Not Yet
  • Shopify is a popular online store platform
  • The version we'd eventually want = $2,300+/mo
  • Way too expensive before we have income
  • Current setup handles everything we need
  • Revisit after 50+ transactions

What is Stripe Connect?

Stripe is a company that handles online payments. Stripe Connect is built specifically for marketplaces — it takes a buyer's payment, holds the money for 7 days while the buyer inspects the equipment, then automatically sends the seller their money and keeps our 7% fee. All of this happens automatically. Cost: 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction.

What is the "7-day hold"?
When a buyer pays, we don't send the money to the seller immediately. We hold it for 7 days so the buyer can receive the equipment, test it, and confirm it matches the listing. If everything is fine, the seller gets paid automatically on day 7. If the buyer has a problem, they can request a return. This is what makes Eye Buy trustworthy for high-ticket purchases — it removes the biggest risk for buyers.

Step 4 — Marketing

Rule #1
Don't run paid ads to an empty marketplace. Get real listings posted first (Jason's job), then drive traffic to them. Free channels first, paid channels second.

Free — Start Immediately

  • Jason's dealer network Jason leads Personal calls to 10 practices Jason already knows. Goal: 5 real listings before spending a dollar on marketing.
  • Facebook group outreach Jason leads Post in optometry equipment Facebook groups. Show the three-price comparison. Hook: "See what your equipment is actually worth vs. what a dealer pays you."
  • LinkedIn posts Both post Optometrists and practice owners are active on LinkedIn. Post data-driven content — "The average spread between trade-in and dealer price is $4,808." No ad spend needed to start.
  • SEO content Raj leads Write articles answering what people Google: "used OCT machine for sale", "what is a used slit lamp worth?" Free traffic that compounds over months. Claude can help write these fast.
  • Email list from day one Raj sets up Add "Get notified when new equipment lists" to the website. Even 50 early subscribers is valuable. Use Mailchimp free tier.

Paid — Use Our Credits First, Then Budget

  • Google Ads — target people searching "used OCT machine for sale", "buy used slit lamp", "sell optometry equipment." High-intent traffic — these people are ready to act. Use our free ad credits first, then ~$300–500/mo once we see what converts.
  • Facebook & Instagram Ads — target optometrists and practice managers by job title. Best creative: show the three-price comparison visually. Test with $50–100 ad sets using credits before spending real money.
Marketing Order of Operations
Start free (Jason's network + Facebook groups) → use ad credits to test → only spend real money once we see what converts. Don't run paid ads to an empty marketplace — get real listings first.
